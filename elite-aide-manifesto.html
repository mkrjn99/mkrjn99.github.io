Elite Aide manifesto [draft]

My dad used to, and till date, runs a small shop. A lot of the work that he did would count as mundane, in my opinion. My dad is a smart guy. I could never understand why he didn't aim higher, why he didn't go out of his way to make his life better. I then decided I never wanted to be that way.

I discovered programming at age of 10. I consider myself lucky enough to be in a position where I was able to realise that programming is nothing but instructing the computer exactly what to do. Human beings since time immemorial have wanted nothing but this: what they want, just occurs. People had slaves for this reason. Kings had a harem full of women for this reason.

When a child wants food and does not have the vocabulary to explain it, he cries, hoping that somebody will give him food. As we grow older, we express our desires through language. Some of those desires are fulfilled while some are not. When our desire is not fulfilled, we feel sad. We feel like we are not in control. A lot of our lives, we just want more control. We feel sad when we think we are not in control. We feel powerless. Powerlessness affects our confidence levels.

Human beings evolved to be social animals. We figured that social structures are good for us. We figured that if we help other people, other people would help us. We evolved to be hierarchical. We evolved in a way that there is a pecking order. In the earlier days, pecking order was hereditary. In the modern world, pecking order, arguably, is defined quantifiably by money. One could argue that it's still hereditary, but it has become easier than ever for one to climb the social pecking order. So I wondered: what is at the bottom of the social pecking order today? And the answer was simple: the computer.

We have created more and more complex computers over the years. The modern LLM is yet another evolution of the idea of a computer. A costly one to build and run for sure, but a good one. One which holds a huge depth of information jampacked in a few gigabytes of storage. One which takes a huge amount of compute resources to run.

As an Indian guy, I have always seen people at the top of the social hierarchy have servants. Servants are much more prominent in India than in the West because the cost of labour is lower in India, compared to the US. One can never define the worth of the human life, but as the society stands today, the median person born in India has lower monetary worth compared to someone born in the West. Considering this difference, a lot of automations that exist in the West would not make commercial sense in India. As an example, in India it is common to have a human being come and wash your utensils every day if you belong to a certain top percentile of the society. While in the West, dishwasher machines are more popular. Everything is a matter of supply and demand.

Some people argue that capitalism has made the rich richer and the poor poorer. I would not get into the debate of whether it is true or not, but I will say this: millions of people of the world today have better lives than kings and queens of the past. Today, sitting in Bangalore, I can think of anything to eat and on the press of a button and miniscule amount of money, someone will come and deliver it at my doorstep. All this was made possible only because of the advancement in science and technology, and the businessmen who had the innovative mindset to leverage it.

Computers are still far from perfect, but they are getting better. At the end of the day, why do we still hire employees? Because AI is far from perfect. There are various job roles today that can be augmented by AI, many arguably replaced as well. But I think that's nothing to worry about. If we do not have to work to sustain a living, it will bring out our true selves. It will unleash the creativity that exists within every single human being since the day they are born. The creativity that is stifled by society. The creativity they suppress in order to make them meet their ends.

When I was working at the lowest wrung of trillion dollar company, I saw senior leadership folks have executive assistants. I wondered what made them so special. Why do they deserve an assistant and I do not? Why can I not delegate the mundane the same way they do? Why can I not just focus on the creative part of my job, the important part of my job and let anyone else take care of the ‚Äúsmaller‚Äù things.

I realised that I was falling into the same mental trap people from the eastern part of the world were already privy to. I was defining work as being ‚Äúsmall‚Äù or ‚Äúbig‚Äù, on the basis of some rubric. This kind of mindset is prevalent in my vicinity anyway. And ironically, I had untrained my brain from thinking this way years ago, by educating myself in ideas like ‚Äúdignity of labour‚Äù. Still, why had I started to think this way again? On introspecting further, I realised that it is because the brain inherently wants to be creative, but the mundane often moves the world forward as it is ‚Äúnecessary‚Äù to further the creative endeavours.

There is a debate on whether the modern LLM is creative or not. But if we go ahead with the assumption that it is not, it belongs correctly at the bottom of the social pecking order. Ideally, most of the mundane tasks of the world should be executed by this layer. But we still have human executive assistants, and arguably, a lot of their jobs is still mundane and not creative. One could argue that being an executive assistant can also be about a person getting mentored from the executive, and that would be a fair goal. But one cannot deny that one is the chief executive of their lives, and hence, we all deserve an assistant of our own. ‚ÄúElite Aide‚Äù aims to be that assistant.

Elite Aide is not a company. it is not a product. It is just an idea. It's a creation of my brain. It's a figment of my imagination. In the real world, it does not exist. But it will. Humans create only that what they can imagine.

Today, I am putting this manifesto out in the world. You are free to build it. I won't sue you, rather, I would laud you. I would love to use the product that you build with this vision. Please build it, I want to use it.
